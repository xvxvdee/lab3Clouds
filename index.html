Clouds

What are clouds?

A cloud is made of water drops or ice crystals floating in the sky. Clouds are created when water vapor, an invisible gas, turns into liquid water droplets. These water droplets form on tiny particles, like dust, that are floating in the air.

Why are clouds White?

Clouds are white because their water droplets or ice crystals are large enough to scatter the light of the seven wavelengths (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet), which combine to produce white light.

Why do clouds float?

A cloud forms when air is heated by the sun. As it rises, it slowly cools it reaches the saturation point and water condenses, forming a cloud. As long as the cloud and the air that its made of is warmer than the outside air around it, it floats!

Types of clouds

Cirrus Clouds
  • Most common
  • Composed of ice and are thin
  • white and predict fair to pleasant weather
  • Indicates that a change in the weather will occur within 24 hours
Alto Clouds
  • Mid Level clouds
  • Composed of ice crystals and water droplets
  • Covers entire sky
  • sun may be dimly visible as a round disk
Stratus Clouds
  • Produces a light mist or drizzle
  • Covers entire sky
  • Grayish
Cumulus Clouds
  • white, puffy, cotton-like
  • Composed of ice and are thin
  • These clouds grow upward and they can develop thunderstorm clouds
  • fair weather
Special Cloud : Fog
  • Cloud on ground
  • Composed of billions of tiny water droplets floating in ari
  • Reduces visibility
